About

I am a PhD Candidate in Operations Research at Rutgers University, working at the intersection of machine learning, mathematical optimization, and quantitative modeling.

My research focuses on integrating predictive models, including neural network surrogates, into large-scale optimization frameworks to enable decision-making under uncertainty. I develop scalable algorithms using decomposition methods such as Benders decomposition and ADMM, with a focus on high-dimensional and scenario-based systems.

I have experience applying these methods to infrastructure planning, energy systems, and data-driven decision problems, combining statistical learning with optimization-based modeling.
